## Context

FRIMO, short for Friend for Modern People, was designed as an application that talks with users like a close companion and automatically records the day as a diary. The product vision combined emotional support, conversational continuity, and lightweight daily reflection.

## Problem

The challenge was to create an integrated conversational experience rather than a single standalone model. The service needed meaningful dialogue generation, emotion awareness, and summarization, all within a development timeline that required a presentable prototype in the middle of a longer product and marketing plan.

## Implementation

I was responsible for the AI side of the project, including model planning, dataset collection and preparation, and model fine-tuning. I used KoGPT2 for response generation, KoBART for summarization and style transfer tasks, and KoBERT for emotion analysis. I also built scripts that allowed these models to be executed from the backend so they could operate as one connected user flow.

## Outcome

The project reached an MVP stage within the required timeline and demonstrated that a multi-model Korean conversational AI service could be organized around practical user needs instead of isolated benchmark tasks. It also became a strong foundation for my later interests in empathetic dialogue and evaluation.
