## Context

Devridge was conceived as an LLM service that could help developers communicate with professionals in different roles. Instead of generic advice, the service aimed to produce feedback that reflected the project description, current situation, and preferred language provided by the user.

## Problem

Cross-functional feedback is valuable but often slow or unevenly distributed. Developers may need design, performance, or code quality suggestions, yet they frequently lack a simple interface for gathering context-aware input from the right perspective at the right time.

## Implementation

The project focused on turning that workflow problem into a usable AI product concept. The service design centered on capturing enough user context to make the generated feedback feel specific rather than generic, then using LLM-based generation to bridge communication between developers and other professional viewpoints.

## Outcome

Devridge demonstrated how LLMs could be used as a structured feedback interface rather than only as a chat novelty. It also expanded my experience in fast prototyping and communicating AI product value to non-specialist audiences.
