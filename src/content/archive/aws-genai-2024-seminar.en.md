Today I attended a seminar in my Advanced AI Topics class by Denis V. Batalov, who serves as a Worldwide Technical Leader for Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence at Amazon Web Services. The talk, titled *GenAI 2024*, gave a broad view of AWS, Generative AI, and Responsible AI.

The presentation drew heavily on the speaker's own path and experience. Stories about indexing books, relocating to AWS in Luxembourg, meeting Professor Youngim Cho at a European event, and working on products such as Kindle Direct Publishing and Amazon Mobile made it easier to understand how long-term exposure to real systems builds technical judgment. Along the way, the talk also touched on themes like Gartner's Hype Cycle for AI and the shift from traditional models to foundation models.

The later part of the talk focused on Responsible AI. Concepts such as fairness and explainability were presented not as abstract ideals, but as practical dimensions of system design. Questions around what counts as bias and how allocation should be judged opened up a more reflective conversation about what "responsible" really means in production contexts. I was also interested in the discussion around explainability methods such as Shapley Value and SHAP, especially as tools for making model predictions interpretable in practice.

The Q&A session was equally useful. When asked which AWS AI/ML service stands out most besides SageMaker, Denis pointed to Bedrock. His explanation made Bedrock sound like a service designed to cover a wider surface area while remaining easier to use, from API access to knowledge-base agents and workflow orchestration.

I also asked whether the English bias of current LLMs could eventually be reduced through technical progress. Denis answered that it could, but also emphasized that English still holds strong power as a common language, which makes region- and culture-specific model development even more important. That answer stayed with me because it connected language equity with real model strategy.

Overall, the seminar was a valuable opportunity to learn not only about GenAI and Responsible AI, but also about how experienced practitioners explain emerging technologies. It also reminded me that I would like to become someone who can eventually speak in public about AI and cloud systems with the same clarity and responsibility.
