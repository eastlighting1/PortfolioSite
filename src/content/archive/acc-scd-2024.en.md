Today I attended ACC SCD, the AWS Campus Club Student Community Day. I remember seeing the promotional post and almost instinctively opening festa.io to sign up.

AWS Cloud Club is a community support program launched by AWS in 2023. This event was actually how I learned about it. Since I had already graduated, I could not join as a student member, which was a little disappointing, but it also made me more determined to stay active through AWSKRUG and through efforts to promote AWS-related activities around me.

ACC SCD 2024 was structured with a wide range of sessions: three career sessions, three architecture sessions, four DevOps sessions, two serverless sessions, and one sponsor session. I attended the introductory architecture session “Architecture Design Starting from Zero,” the advanced architecture session “There Is a Reason Behind the Cloud Native Trend,” the serverless session “Lambda and EventBridge Give You Wings,” the DevOps session “Building a Scalable 3-Tier Architecture and CI/CD with Terraform,” and the sponsor session “Exploring the Notion API with AWS.”

The architecture design session and the Terraform session were both framed around the speaker’s personal experience, which made them especially relatable because I had touched similar topics myself the previous winter. The Notion API session was also helpful because I had already felt some limitations with the currently available integrations. The session that interested me most, though, was the one on Lambda and EventBridge. The idea that Lambda triggers can connect naturally to services like S3 or SQS, and that EventBridge rules can be arranged to shape behavior exactly as needed, felt like something that could be very useful in my future work.

Overall, the event felt full and worthwhile from the keynote to the closing activities. It was a bit unfortunate that there was no separate networking session, because that meant the day focused more on knowledge than on direct interaction among students interested in AWS. Still, that feels like something the event series could improve over time. Since most AWS Cloud Club activities are limited to students from participating universities, it may be hard to witness the broader history of the community outside of events like Community Day, but I would love to someday meet ACC members again as either a speaker or a staff member at an AWS event.
