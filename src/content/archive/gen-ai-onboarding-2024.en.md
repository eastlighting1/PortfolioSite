Today I attended Gen AI Onboarding, hosted by Wrtn Technologies and GDSC, and had the chance to listen to several excellent talks.

Wrtn Technologies runs the generative AI platform service “Wrtn,” which aims to make generative AI easy for anyone to use. I was genuinely surprised to hear that the company, founded in 2023, had reached one million users in only seven months. Since I had only tried the service briefly before, I found myself thinking, “I should have kept using it.”

The event was organized into two sessions: Prompt Specialist, which focused on prompt engineering guidance, and Gen AI Career, in which engineers from Wrtn answered questions about working in generative AI. In the Prompt Specialist session, Wrtn’s DevRel, Chedda, introduced a range of practical ideas, from Chain of Thought to broader prompting processes and a set of seven useful tips. It felt like a very good opportunity to see prompt engineering presented as a craft rather than as a vague buzzword.

The Gen AI Career session featured both a platform engineer and an ML engineer. They answered pre-submitted questions with a level of depth that stayed with me long after the event, and the live questions from other attendees also made the session especially valuable. I was particularly grateful for the answers they gave to questions related to roles I am personally interested in.

The three hours passed surprisingly quickly. I would have liked to spend more time networking with the practicing engineers and the participants who asked such sharp questions, but I had to head home, which left me a little disappointed.
