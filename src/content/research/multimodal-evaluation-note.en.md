## Summary

This technical note reflects implementation-oriented evaluation work carried out in a human-centered multimodal AI project. It focuses less on introducing a new model and more on turning evaluation requirements into code and reviewable outputs.

## Why It Matters

Research projects often define evaluation goals abstractly, but project delivery depends on whether those goals can be implemented consistently. This kind of work sits at the boundary between research planning and engineering execution.

## Contribution

The note documents the practical value of evaluation code, reproducible outputs, and implementation discipline in collaborative AI research settings.
